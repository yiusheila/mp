AAPM TG-43 Factors Considered
  1. Dose-rate Constant
  2. Specific Source Design
  3. Consensus Dosimetry Data
    • measured dose-rate distribution using LiF TLD
    • Monte Carlo calculation from literatures
  4. Encapsulated Source
  5. Source Geometry
    • Longitudinal Cut = Seed = cylindrical source with effective length Leff ≤ 0.5 cm
    • Transverse Cut = Point Source = Dimensionless source with Spherical Dose Distribution
      • inverse square law applied

Not Considering
  1. Applicator
  2. Attenuation caused by other sources
  3. Transit Dose
  4. Inhomogeneity
  5. β/n emitting sources

Why need TG-43
  • The relapse-free survival rate is correlated with dose (100 - 160 Gy)
    • accurate dosimetry is crucial

\( S_K = \dot{K}_\delta(d) ~d^2 \)
To measure the SK of a source,
  1. place a detector at a distance from the centre of the source (on the perpendicular bisector plane), so that the source can be consider as point source
  2. measure Air Kerma Rate in vacuo (or corrected for air attenuation and scattering) [1]
  3. take away the contribution of low energy and contamination photons from the reading (usually < 5 keV)
  4. multiple the distance d2 with the net reading
