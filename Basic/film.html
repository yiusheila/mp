Radiochromic Film

Background Active component:
diacetylene monomers (e.g. Li-PCDA [10,12-pentacosadiynoic acid])
(vs radiographic film: silver halide based)

Mechanism:
polymerisation of the diacetylene monomer
 →  blue coloured polymer
 →  i.e. absorb red + green

Design:
  1. ↑ diacetylene crystal size
    ↑ scatter of transmitted light
    ∴ ↓ spatial resolution
      but ↑ sensitivity
    ↓ diacetylene crystal size
    ↓ sensitivity
    but ↑ contrast at higher dose
    ∵ steeper slope of dose response curve
  2. yellow marker dye
    added to active emulsion layer
    →
    absorb blue light

    → proportional to the layer thickness
    ← since thicker layer → more active component per unit area → more absorption
  3. the substrate layers were made rough with silica particles
    → ↓ reflection of light between two smooth surface
    → prevent
    Newton's rings interference
  4. almost energy independent after MeV range down to ~40 keV
  5. tissue equivalent

Advantages:
:) no film processing
:)  ↑ resolution, ↑ dynamic range
:)  ↓ sensitive to visible light > no dark room
:) Dose rate independent

* store at room temperature or lower
* keep relative humidity ↓

OD-Pixel Value relationship
\( OD = log_{10} \Large \frac{2^{16}}{PV} \), for 16-bit scan per channel i.e. 48-bit RGB
NIH Rodbard function for fitting: \( D = c \Large \left( \frac{OD-a}{d-OD}\right) ^\frac{1}{b} \)

  1. Film model + lot
  2. Type of radiation + energy
  3. Readout system
    Film orientation Transmission/reflection mode
    Temperature of scanner glass
    Correction
  4. Time gap between irradiation and readout
    ← due to post exposure growth

active layer
 >

Properties
  • Exposure Range = 0.01 - 10 mGy
  • Optical Density
    $$ \begin{align} OD &= log_{10} \left( \frac{I_0}{I_t} \right) \\ &= log_{10} \left( \frac{1}{T} \right) \end{align} $$
  • Characteristic Curve
     = OD vs Log(Exposure) Curve
  • Reciprocity Law
    Film Response = Exposure × Time
  • Film Density
     = film darkness
  • Base Density
     = Inherent OD with no exposure
     ~ 0.1
  • Fog Density
     = Contaminated OD by
    • Accident exposure during storage
    • Chemical contamination
    • Improper processing
     < 0.1
  • Film Speed
     = Sensitivity to Radiation/Light
     | slow = more exposure \[ Speed = { 1 \over Exposure } \]
