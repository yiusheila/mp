Standard Error of the Mean (SEM)  = repeatedly taking samples from a population
the SD of the sample distribution
\[ SEM = \frac{s}{\sqrt{n}} \]
where
s = sample SD
Variation of Sampling Data Coefficient of Variation (CV) \[ CV = \frac{SD}{mean} \]
Variance
\[ variance = SD^2 \]
Confidence Interval \[ 95\% ~CI ~range = Sample~Mean \pm 1.96 \times SE \]

95% CI = when repeat the study many times,
the true population mean will be with in the range in 95% occasion
