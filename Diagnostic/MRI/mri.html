Larmour Frequency Magnetic moment \(\vec{\mu}\) under an external \(B_0\) field, a torque \(\vec{T}\) is produced \[ \vec{T} = \frac{d \vec{\mu}}{dt} = \vec{\mu} \times \gamma \vec{B_0} \] the solution to this equation describes the precession of \(\vec{\mu}\) about the direction of \(B_0\) at an angular frequency ω \[ \omega = \gamma B \]
where
ω = Larmour/precession/resonance frequency (MHz)
γ = gyromagnetic ratio
B = magnetic field strength (T)


e.g. γ of
1H
= 42.6 MHz T-1
  ∴ the resonance frequency ω
@ 1.5 T
= (42.6 MHz T-1)(1.5 T) ~
64 MHz



Wavelength of RF in Water
for 1H @ 1.5 T,
$$ \begin{align} \lambda_{H} &= \frac{c}{f} \\ &= \frac{3 \times 10^8 ~ms^{-1} }{64 \times 10^6~Hz} \\ &= 4.70~m \end{align} $$
When the 1H is in water,
we should consider the
dielectric constant
of water, since the RF wave is travelling in a dielectric medium,
given εwater = 81
,
$$ \begin{align} \lambda_{H, w} &= \frac{\lambda_H}{\sqrt{\epsilon}} \\ &= \frac{4.70~m}{\sqrt{81}} \\ &= 0.52~m \\ &= 52~cm \end{align} $$
